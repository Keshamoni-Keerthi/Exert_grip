Herbal Remedies for Cold and Cough

Tulsi (Holy Basil, Ocimum sanctum)

Benefits:

  • Contains antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that relieve respiratory infections.
  • Helps clear mucus and soothes the throat.

Usage:

  • Brew tulsi leaves in water to make tea.
  • Add honey and ginger to enhance its effectiveness.

Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Benefits:

  • Reduces throat irritation and cough due to its anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Acts as a natural expectorant, helping to expel mucus.

Usage:

  • Boil fresh ginger slices in water to make tea.
  • Mix ginger juice with honey for a soothing syrup.

Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

Benefits:

  • Contains menthol, which relieves nasal congestion and soothes irritated airways.
  • Helps with dry cough and sinus discomfort.

Usage:

  • Drink peppermint tea or inhale steam infused with peppermint oil.

Licorice Root (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Benefits:

  • Soothes sore throat and reduces coughing.
  • Has antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties to support respiratory health.

Usage:

  • Prepare licorice tea by boiling the root in water.
  • Use licorice lozenges for instant relief.

Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus)

Benefits:

  • Acts as a natural decongestant, helping to clear blocked airways.
  • Relieves cold symptoms by reducing inflammation and opening nasal passages.

Usage:

  • Inhale steam with a few drops of eucalyptus oil.
  • Use eucalyptus oil in a diffuser for consistent relief.
